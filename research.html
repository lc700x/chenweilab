Molecular-scale Interface Engineering for 2D-materials based electronic and optoelectronic devices for neuromorphic computing, and Interface-Controlled Nanocatalysis for Energy and Environmental Research.

Many technologically important devices, such as transistors, memories, lasers, solar cells, light emitting diodes, sensors, electrochemical energy storage devices, all exploit interfacial phenomena. Without precise control of the interface properties, many devices will not function properly. The broad objective of Dr. Chen Wei’s research is to have molecular-scale understanding of these interface properties, and hence to provide design rules for effective interface engineering approaches to improve device performance and materials efficiency, with particular emphasis on the interface engineering for 2D-materials based electronic and optoelectronic devices for neuromorphic computing, and interface-controlled nanocatalyst for energy and environmental research.

Current and future research interests:

  • (1) Interface-controlled nanocatalysis for energy and environmental research
  • (2) Molecular-level interface engineering for 2D-materials based electronic and optoelectronic devices for neuromorphic computing
  • (3) Rational design of self-assembled molecular nanostructure arrays over macroscopic area with superior multi-functionalities for molecular nano-devices

3. Rational design of self-assembled molecular nanostructure arrays over macroscopic area with superior multi-functionalities for molecular nano-devices
